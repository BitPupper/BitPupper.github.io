I wanted to feature this because I think it is a great skit that illustrates kind of rural life I experienced growing up in Akita prefecture.
https://www.instagram.com/reel/DaU5UYTvVKJ/?utm_source=ig_web_button_share_sheet&igsh=MzRlODBiNWFlZA==

A core memory the video triggered was when I was catching bugs and playing with mud around a sewer like this with a group of kids from my school playing soccer nearby. Someone accidentally kicked the ball into the sewer and they asked me to get it. I sat down on the edge and leaned backwards for balance and got the ball between my feet. They were so excited to get the ball back that one of them immediately kicked it and it smashed right into my face. I literally saw stars. An old lady working on the hatake nearby heard me cry and the kids panicking asking if I'm okay and she dragged me to a manual water pump to wash off the mud that the ball left on me.
A few years ago, I would have considered a memory like this too mundane or embarrassing to even publicly post about. But living in New York has made me re-evaluate the nature and the peace with no life threatening stakes constantly looming over. I love both experiences. Being able to struggle in the US taught me to treasure each and every waking moment I have. And my roots in Akita help me to ground myself and remember that there is more to life than the rat race and competition.
